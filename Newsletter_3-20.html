9 Steps to Buying a Home
1. Check Your Credit
Get familiar with where you are financially. Check both your credit score and report with all three credit bureaus. If you are not paying with cash, your credit score will partly determine which kind of mortgage you qualify for.
2. Determine Your Budget
Once you know where your credit stands, consider your income and how much of it you’d like to spend on your home monthly and over time. This step is also where you begin to get familiar with your local housing market. Browse some of my listings to see what you can get for what price in the area you plan to buy in. Consider making a list of “musts” & “negotiables” at this step as well once you are familiar with what you can afford.
3. Get Pre-Approved by a Lender
You have an idea of what you’d like to pay for your home. Now is the time to find out what a mortgage lender will approve you for. There is a distinction between a “pre-qualification” and a “pre-approval”. Here we want a pre-approval. A pre-approval is a promise from a lender that you will be approved for a home up to a certain amount. While a pre-qualification is just an idea of what you might qualify for. There is no commitment. I can connect you with a great local lender when you’re ready for this step.
4. Start Looking at Homes
Now that you know how much of a mortgage you will be approved for; you want to start your serious search. With the list you’ve created of your “musts” & “negotiables” along with your pre-approval, we can start to find your dream home.
5. Make An Offer
Once we’ve found your dream home, we’ll make an offer.
6. Home Inspection
After your offer has been accepted, we can schedule the home inspection and check with the local municipality to make sure there are no pending legal matters associated with the property.
7. Get Insurance & Transfer Utilities
Once our inspections check out, you’ll shop for insurance and get your utilities in the home situated and ready for your move in!
8. Close!
It’s closing day!
9. Move In!
